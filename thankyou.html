Important Information for you:
The healthcare system in each EU country operates differently, therefore we would advise you to check with the health service in the relevant countries through which you plan to travel to see what is covered by the EHIC.

A 'patient contribution' (also known as a 'co-payment') is required in some EU countries. In some cases it is possible to claim a refund for these charges either in the country in which the treatment has taken place or when you return to the UK. The EHIC should cover any emergency medical treatment required whilst your are within the EU as a result of illness or an accident.

For emergency assistance whilst in the EU, please dial 112 from any telephone or mobile phone.

EHIC cover includes treatment for chronic or pre-existing medical conditions that require urgent attention during your visit to any EU country. Whilst this is upheld on the vast majority of occasions, we do recommend checking with the relevant EU healthcare service(s) prior to travelling. Your EHIC is not a replacement for medical insurance.

Travelling abroad with the intention of giving birth is not covered by the EHIC however, should a birth happen unexpectedly the EHIC will cover all medical treatment for mother and baby relating to the birth.

For treatment relating to oxygen and kidney dialysis you will need to pre-book any foreseen treatment or assistance. Please contact your GP for advice and ensure that this is not covered by private medical healthcare, otherwise this will not be covered by the EHIC.
